Screening a Go backend dev (3–4 yrs)

Twenty questions with full answer guidelines — what a strong answer covers, strong signals vs red flags, follow-up probes, and a 1–5 scoring bar per question. Built for you as the interviewer: 5 years of fullstack experience, Go depth still growing.

How this works: nothing is date-locked — interviews don't wait for a schedule. Before a screen: skim Part 6 (the playbook), pick 8–10 questions across Parts 1–5, print the scorecard. Each question card tells you exactly what to listen for, so you can evaluate answers deeper than your own current Go experience. Side effect: studying the answer guidelines is itself excellent Go study — they cover the same ground as your Go Intensive, from the other side of the table.

Always open — per-candidate tools

The scorecard is the one-page version of the whole kit: every question, its 3/5 and 5/5 bars, and the decision rubric. Print one per candidate.

Quick pre-interview ritual (15 minutes)

  • Read Part 6 — the 45-minute structure, red flags, decision bar.
  • Pick your questions: 2 fundamentals · 2–3 concurrency · 1–2 backend/data · 1 testing · always Q19 + Q20.
  • Do the self-check quizzes in Part 1 and Part 2 — you'll probe better when the answers are fresh.
  • Print the scorecard.
